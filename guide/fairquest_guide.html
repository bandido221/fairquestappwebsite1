Fair Owner & Vendor Guide

How FairQuest Works

A complete guide for fair owners and vendors — from setting up your fair to receiving applications, tracking payments, and managing your vendor directory.

PART 1 — FOR THE FAIR OWNER

Setting Up Your Fair on FairQuest

Once your fair is added to FairQuest and set up as a Gold tier fair, you'll have access to a full admin panel. Here's how to get everything ready before your vendors start applying.

1

Log In & Go to Your Dashboard

Log into FairQuest with your account. From the main menu go to My Fairs, then click on your fair to open your Fair Dashboard. This is your main control center for everything — fair info, applications, vendors, and payments.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Fair Dashboard main page
2

Update Your Fair Information

Click Update Fair to fill in your fair's details — name, address, dates for each weekend, hours of operation, description, and your fair image. This is what vendors and attendees will see on your public FairQuest page. Make sure your weekend dates are correct — they are used for pricing and payment tracking.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Update Fair page showing fields and weekend dates
3

Set Your Weekend Pricing

Go to Vendor & Performer Directory → Manage Weekend Pricing. For each weekend you added, set three prices:

🧺 Artisan / Craft Vendors

The price vendors pay your fair per weekend

🍖 Food Vendors

The price food vendors pay your fair per weekend (usually different from artisan vendors)

🎭 Performers

The fee your fair pays performers per weekend

📅 3-Day Weekends

You can set a different price for longer weekends — the system shows the number of days automatically

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Weekend Pricing page
4

Create Your Application Forms

Go to Applications → Manage Application Forms. You can create one form for each type: Artisan/Craft Vendors, Food Vendors, and Performers. Each form has its own questions and contract. You write your vendor rules and terms in plain text — vendors must read and agree before they can submit.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Manage Application Forms page showing the three form types
🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the form editor showing custom questions
5

Tell Your Vendors to Create a FairQuest Account & Apply

Once your forms are ready, tell your vendors and performers to create a free FairQuest account. Once they log in, they can find your fair, click Apply, and submit their application directly through the system. Their profile information is pulled automatically — they just answer your custom questions, pick which weekends they want to attend, read and agree to your contract, and submit.

📢 What to tell your vendors

"Create a free account at fairquest.com, search for our fair, and click Apply. Your contact info will be filled in automatically. Just answer the questions, pick your weekends, and agree to the contract. That's it!"

PART 2 — REVIEWING APPLICATIONS

Managing Incoming Applications

Once vendors start applying, you'll see everything in your Applications page. Here's how to review and manage them.

1

View All Applications

From your dashboard click Applications. You'll see all submissions grouped by type — Artisan Vendors, Food Vendors, and Performers. Each row shows the applicant's name, status, and when they applied. Use the search and filters to find specific applicants quickly.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Applications list page
2

Review Each Application

Click Review Application on any row to see the full details — their answers, uploaded files, mailing address, and whether they accepted the contract. You can approve or decline from this page.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Application Detail page
3

Application Summary on Dashboard

Your dashboard always shows a live summary — how many vendors are in review, approved, or declined, broken down by vendor type. So you can see at a glance where things stand.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Applications Summary section on the dashboard
PART 3 — VENDOR DIRECTORY

Managing Your Vendor & Performer Directory

The Vendor & Performer Directory is your master list of everyone who has applied to your fair. It tracks who's new, who's returning, and who you want to invite back next year.

1

New vs Returning Applicants

Every applicant is automatically tagged as 🆕 New or 🔄 Returning. The system tracks how many years they've applied to your fair. For your first year on FairQuest, you can manually set the correct number of years for vendors who have been with you before the system existed. After that, it updates automatically every year.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Vendor Directory showing new/returning badges and years count
2

Set Invite Status for Next Year

For each vendor or performer, you can set their invite status:

✅ Will Invite

This person will get an invitation to apply again next year

🤔 Maybe

Undecided — you can review and decide later

❌ Will Not Invite

This person will not receive an invitation. Add a note explaining why for your own records.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of a vendor row showing invite status dropdown and notes field
3

Add Notes Per Vendor

Add private notes to any vendor or performer — only your admin team can see these. Use them to track important details like "great vendor, always on time" or "late payment last year". All changes are logged automatically with who made the change and when.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the notes field on a vendor row
PART 4 — PAYMENT TRACKING

Tracking Payments

FairQuest does not process payments directly — vendors pay you through PayPal or however you prefer. But FairQuest gives you a fast way to track who has paid, for which weekends, and save the PayPal reference.

1

Payment Tracking Page

Go to Payment Tracking from your dashboard. You'll see every vendor and performer with a row for each weekend they signed up for. Each row shows how much is due and the current payment status.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the Payment Tracking page
2

Mark a Payment as Received

When a vendor sends payment (e.g. via PayPal), search for them by name or email. Enter the amount paid, paste the PayPal reference or note, change the status to ✅ Paid, and click Save. The system records who marked it paid and when.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of marking a payment as paid with PayPal reference
3

Performers Are Different

For performers, the payment direction is reversed — your fair pays them, not the other way around. The payment tracking page shows this clearly, with "Fee" instead of "Due" and tracks how much your fair has paid out to each performer per weekend.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of a performer payment row showing fee owed by fair
PART 5 — FOR VENDORS & PERFORMERS

How Vendors & Performers Apply

This section explains the vendor experience — what they see and how they apply to your fair.

1

Create a FairQuest Account

Vendors and performers go to fairquest.com and create a free account. They fill in their profile — business name, description, photos, contact info, and type (vendor, food vendor, or performer). This profile is used across all the fairs they apply to.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the vendor profile / account creation page
2

Find the Fair & Click Apply

Once logged in, the vendor finds your fair on FairQuest. Gold tier fairs show an Apply button. They click it and the application opens — their contact info and profile details are already filled in automatically.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the fair listing page showing the Apply button
3

Fill Out the Application

The vendor answers your custom questions, selects which weekends they want to attend, reads and accepts your contract, then submits. The whole process takes just a few minutes.

🖼️ ADD IMAGE HERE Screenshot of the vendor application form with weekend picker and contract
4

Fair Admin Reviews & Approves

After the vendor submits, the application appears in your admin Applications page. You review it, approve or decline, and the vendor gets notified. Once approved, they appear in your Vendor Directory and payment tracking automatically.
